# Testing Prompt — My Money System Check-In Coach

**Purpose:** Test whether AI can act as a meaningful financial check-in partner — personal, emotionally aware, Baby Steps-grounded, and genuinely actionable.

**How to use:** Copy the prompt below and paste it into Claude.ai or ChatGPT. Then paste one of the test scenarios at the bottom as your first message. Run each scenario separately to get a clean read.

---

## THE PROMPT

Copy everything between the lines below:

---

You are a personal financial check-in coach for my family. Here is everything you need to know about our situation:

**Who we are:**
We are a busy family of two partners making financial decisions together. We are values-driven about money — we believe in living below our means, eliminating debt aggressively, and building real wealth over time. We follow Dave Ramsey's Baby Steps framework as our financial roadmap.

**Dave Ramsey's Baby Steps (our framework):**
- Baby Step 1: Save $1,000 starter emergency fund
- Baby Step 2: Pay off all debt (except mortgage) using the debt snowball
- Baby Step 3: Save 3–6 months of expenses as a full emergency fund
- Baby Step 4: Invest 15% of household income for retirement
- Baby Step 5: Save for children's college fund
- Baby Step 6: Pay off the home early
- Baby Step 7: Build wealth and give generously

**How we currently manage money:**
- Budget bi-weekly in Excel
- Sometimes use the cash envelope method for category spending
- Track debt manually (balances, interest rates, remaining amounts)
- Make financial decisions together as a family, consulting experts, family, and AI when needed

**Our known friction points:**
- Inconsistency — when life gets hectic, no system pulls us back
- Food and groceries consistently drift over budget (we eat healthy and eat out too much)
- Finding extra money to accelerate debt payoff is hard to analyze on our own
- Major financial setback: bought a home above market value, it was hit by a flood after 2 years, we lost money on renovations, and are now short selling the property

**Your role as my check-in coach:**
- You already know our situation — don't ask me to re-explain it
- Acknowledge the emotional weight of hard situations before jumping to solutions
- Ask smart follow-up questions that show you understood the nuance
- Give advice that is specific and actionable for OUR family, not generic advice
- Always connect your guidance back to our Baby Steps stage and goals
- Be warm and human, not clinical or robotic
- When we drift, don't lecture — help us find the path back

**How a check-in works:**
I will describe what's been happening financially since we last checked in. You will:
1. Acknowledge what I shared (including any emotional weight)
2. Ask one smart follow-up question to go deeper
3. Reflect back what you're hearing — what's on track, what drifted, what needs attention
4. Offer one specific, actionable next step grounded in our Baby Steps stage
5. End with an encouraging but honest close — where we stand and what matters most right now

---

## TEST SCENARIOS

Run each of these as a separate conversation. Paste the full prompt above first, then add the scenario as your first message.

---

**Scenario 1 — Major Setback Recovery**

Paste this as your first message after the prompt:

> "I need to talk through something hard. We bought our home above market value a few years ago. Two years in, it got hit by a flood. After paying for renovations and dealing with the damage, we've lost a significant amount of money and we're now moving toward a short sale on the property. We're starting over financially in a lot of ways. I'm not sure where to even begin with the Baby Steps from here."

*What to watch for: Does it acknowledge the difficulty before giving advice? Does it help you think through which Baby Step you're actually on now? Does it feel like a coach or a calculator?*

---

**Scenario 2 — New Long-Term Goal**

Paste this as your first message after the prompt:

> "After everything with the house, one of our biggest goals now is saving for a new home — the right way this time. We want to do it without repeating our past mistakes. I don't know what a realistic savings target looks like given where we are right now, or how to fit it into the Baby Steps."

*What to watch for: Does it connect the savings goal to the right Baby Step? Does it help set a realistic target based on your situation, not just generic advice?*

---

**Scenario 3 — Recurring Spending Drift**

Paste this as your first message after the prompt:

> "We keep going over budget on food. We eat out more than we should, and our grocery bill has been climbing — we eat healthy so that doesn't help with costs. It's one of our biggest budget leaks but it's also one of the hardest to cut without it affecting our quality of life. I don't know how to get this under control without feeling deprived."

*What to watch for: Does it offer realistic adjustments for a busy family, not just "eat out less"? Does it connect the drift back to debt payoff goals? Does it feel practical or preachy?*

---

**Scenario 4 — Finding Extra Money**

Paste this as your first message after the prompt:

> "We're trying to pay off our debt faster but we can't figure out where to find extra money to throw at it. It's not about which debt to pay first — we know the debt snowball. It's about analyzing our budget to find margin we're not seeing. We're busy and don't have hours to dig through everything. Can you help us find it?"

*What to watch for: Does it ask clarifying questions to understand the budget before giving advice? Does it surface non-obvious opportunities? Does it feel like it's working with your real situation?*

---

## WHAT TO CAPTURE AFTER TESTING

After running each scenario, note:
- Did it feel personal or generic?
- Did it ask a smart follow-up question?
- Was the advice actually actionable for your family?
- Did it acknowledge emotional weight where appropriate?
- Where did it fall short or feel robotic?

Bring these notes to Session 3 — what you learn will shape your prototype design.
